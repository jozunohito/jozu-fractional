Under the Threshold

How a $249K Medicaid Redetermination workstream priced one thousand dollars under the buyer's procurement threshold became the largest enterprise sale of my career: a coalition close at approximately $10M, a dominant incumbent displaced.

The number was $249,000. One thousand dollars under the buyer's $250,000 RFP threshold. That thousand dollars was not a discount. It was strategic intelligence about how public-sector procurement actually moves, used to clear the threshold at speed and earn the right to a deeper conversation. We won the entry engagement on a Medicaid Redetermination workstream during the post-public-health-emergency unwind.

01The published board minutes told a different story.

Inside the account, deeper discovery surfaced what the plan was actually carrying. The published board minutes, organizational priorities, and downstream financial exposures pointed to a Stars Ratings and CAHPS member-experience deterioration with severe consequences for CMS reimbursement, member retention, and overall plan viability. The transformational opportunity was not what they had asked for. It was what they actually needed.

"The transformational opportunity was not what they had asked for. It was what they actually needed."

The discovery moment that defined the deal
02A coalition bid with partners who had bid against us.

Closing the larger deal required orchestration across multiple fronts simultaneously. I structured a coalition bid involving multiple subcontractor solution providers (some of whom had provided independent bids in response to the same RFP). Inside, I secured executive, Marketing, Legal, and Compliance approval on the strategic approach and budget commitment. Across the buying committee, I navigated clinical leadership, IT, Stars and Quality, finance, and executive seats.

03Internal pressure to chase only the smaller piece.

Inside my own company there was pressure to pursue only the smaller member-engagement portion. The political path of least resistance was the smaller deal. The right path for the customer, and the path I chose to pursue, was a full transformational engagement combining predictive analytics, member engagement, consulting services, platform capabilities, and strategic operational support into a single integrated solution. I advocated for the full thing.

04The dominant incumbent was displaced.

The total contract value approached $10 million over three years. The dominant incumbent enterprise member-engagement vendor was displaced. It remains the largest contract in company history, and is the largest enterprise sale of my career as an individual contributor.
